00 / Context

NetEase's flagship action PC title going global — and the team that runs that operation.

NARAKA: BLADEPOINT is a 60-player melee-focused battle royale built on a Wuxia combat system. Developed by 24 Entertainment, published globally by NetEase Leihuo. The game has crossed 20M+ players worldwide since its 2021 Steam launch, and continues to ship major content updates, esports events, and seasonal campaigns.

My role sits inside the overseas marketing team — a small, fast-moving group that owns the official channels on X (Twitter), Reddit, Discord, YouTube, and Twitch. We ship multilingual content, run creator programs, and translate the game's Wuxia identity for global audiences without losing its edge.

What makes this internship different from a typical overseas social role: I'm not just executing on a calendar. I independently own a platform, run weekly research, and pitch creative concepts that actually get shot. The AI workflow piece is the multiplier.

// Challenge

The overseas player base is geographically distributed and culturally fragmented. Anecdote-based decisions were slow and biased. We needed a single source of truth for what players actually think, week over week.

03 / 03
Reflection

What I'd do differently. What I learned.

Honest notes on the gaps, the wins, and the one thing I'd tell my first-day self.

What I'd do differently

  1. Set up Reddit data tracking and KPI dashboards from week 1, not week 6
  2. Treat the AI prompt library as a team artifact, not a personal one — share it earlier
  3. Push back on translation-only workflows earlier; bilingual co-creation is faster than sequential
  4. Build creator relationships directly, not through layers of internal process

What I learned

  1. Overseas hard-core communities reward authenticity and punish corporate tone — every post earns or loses trust
  2. AI is an accelerator, not a substitute. The judgment of what to ship still belongs to a human
  3. Cross-time-zone team coordination is a creative constraint, not a limitation — better briefs > more meetings
  4. The best marketing work comes from a player who can also write code
